Director's Cockpit — Quick Start Guide

Welcome. This document walks you through the demo at:

  https://demo-v2-steel.vercel.app/

Read time: about five minutes. After that, you'll be able to take any
brief from idea to finished ad assets in roughly the same amount of
time again.


================================================================
WHAT THIS DEMO DOES
================================================================

You give three lines of brief. The demo produces four marketing
assets, in this order:

  1. Ad copy        (headline, caption, call-to-action)
  2. Image          (a generated photograph for the ad)
  3. Script         (a voiceover script, 20-40 seconds)
  4. Audio          (the script, read aloud by the voice you pick)

At each step you see two options. You either pick one, ask for two
more, or describe a refinement in plain English ("more aggressive,"
"lighter background," "the person should smile more"). You never
write a prompt. You direct.

When all four steps are approved, the demo assembles a Final Package
with a one-click zip download containing every asset plus a
Director's Notes summary of every decision you made.


================================================================
TWO WAYS TO START
================================================================

OPTION A — The Quick Demo (no setup, sub-second)

On the welcome screen, click "Try the sample brief instead." A
pre-built run loads instantly. You'll see the entire pipeline restore
from cache: copy, image, script, voice, and audio all approved in
under a second, then the Final Package appears.

Use this to see the shape of the tool before doing your own brief.
No API keys needed. Zero network calls.


OPTION B — Your Own Brief (requires four API keys)

To run a real pipeline on your own product, you need to provide four
API keys. The demo never sends them to any server other than the
service each key belongs to, and they clear from your browser when
you close the tab.

Click "Open Settings" on the welcome screen. Paste the four keys.
Click "Validate keys." When all four show a green check, close the
Settings drawer and the brief form will appear.


================================================================
THE FOUR API KEYS YOU'LL NEED
================================================================

The keys are free or low-cost to sign up for. Per-session usage on a
typical brief runs under a few cents total.

  OpenAI        https://platform.openai.com/api-keys
    Used by:    copy generation (fallback), script generation, image
                prompt builder, direction translator
    Note:       starts with "sk-..."

  fal.ai        https://fal.ai/dashboard/keys
    Used by:    image generation (Flux Schnell)
    Note:       format is a UUID, a colon, and a hex string, all
                pasted as one value. Make sure you copy the full key
                including everything after the colon.

  ElevenLabs    https://elevenlabs.io/app/settings/api-keys
    Used by:    voice synthesis on the audio step
    Note:       you also need to have at least one voice in your
                ElevenLabs voice library — visit
                https://elevenlabs.io/app/voicelab to confirm

  Anthropic     https://console.anthropic.com/settings/keys
    Used by:    higher-quality copy generation (Claude Sonnet) and
                the optional image critique feature
    Note:       starts with "sk-ant-..."

If you skip the Anthropic key, the demo still works — copy generation
falls back to OpenAI's smaller model and the image Critique button is
disabled. Everything else functions normally.


================================================================
ENTERING YOUR KEYS
================================================================

  1. Click "Open Settings" in the welcome screen (or click "Settings"
     in the top-right corner at any time).

  2. The Settings drawer slides in from the right with four password
     fields, one per service.

  3. Paste each key into its corresponding field. The values are
     hidden as you type (just like a password).

  4. Click "Validate keys" at the bottom of the drawer. Each field
     will show one of:

         a green check  — the key was accepted
         a red mark     — the key was rejected (re-check it)
         a dash         — not yet validated

  5. When all four show green checks, click "Close" at the top right
     of the drawer.

A tip: if a key shows red but you're sure you copied it correctly,
clear the field, then paste again — sometimes a stray space or
newline gets copied with the key.


================================================================
THE BRIEF FORM
================================================================

Three fields:

  Product name      — what you're advertising
  Target audience   — who you're advertising to
  Ad angle          — the message or promise

Examples:

  Product name:    Strata Coffee
  Target audience: early-shift workers, 25 to 40
  Ad angle:        the first sip is the start of the win

Click "Start." The copy step begins generating immediately.


================================================================
STEP 1: COPY
================================================================

After about two seconds, two copy variants appear, side by side. Each
has:

  Headline   — 6 to 10 words
  Caption    — 2 to 3 sentences in Meta or Instagram tone
  CTA        — 3 to 5 words, action-led

Three things you can do:

  Pick one         — click the dark "Pick this" button on the variant
                     you like. The image step begins immediately.

  Show me 2 more   — see two additional variants alongside the first
                     two. The first two stay visible. Repeat as often
                     as you like.

  Refine           — type a direction in the textarea, then click
                     Refine. Two NEW variants replace the current
                     ones. Try:

                         "more aggressive, less corporate"
                         "softer, more conversational"
                         "make it pop"
                         "playful, light"

When you pick a variant, the image step starts.


================================================================
STEP 2: IMAGE
================================================================

About three seconds after you approve copy, two images appear. The
ad copy you picked is woven into the image — the headline's promise
is what the image is selling.

Three things to do:

  Pick one         — click "Pick this." The script step begins.

  Critique         — click "Critique" on any image. A creative-
                     director review appears in 2 to 4 seconds:

                         "The dashboard placement is strong — it
                         draws the eye to the product first. The
                         lighting reads stock-photo though, soft and
                         even across the frame, which kills the
                         urgency the headline is selling. Try a
                         single warm key light from the right and
                         let the background fall into shadow; same
                         composition, much more weight."

                     Below the critique, an "Apply this critique"
                     button. Clicking it generates two new images
                     that address what the critique pointed out.

  Refine           — same as copy step. Type a direction:

                         "lighter background, more energy"
                         "more cinematic"
                         "darker, more dramatic"
                         "the person should be smiling"

The Critique feature requires the Anthropic API key. Without it,
the button is disabled.


================================================================
STEP 3: SCRIPT + VOICE
================================================================

This step has two phases.

PHASE A — Pick a script

Two script variants appear. Each is 50 to 100 words, designed for
spoken delivery — short clauses, natural breath points, no corporate
hedging. Read one out loud to make sure it sounds right in your
voice.

Pick one. Refine in plain English if needed:

    "more urgent"
    "calmer, more grounded"
    "punchier"


PHASE B — Pick a voice

After picking a script, a panel appears with voices from your
ElevenLabs account. Each card shows the voice's name and category.

  Click the play icon on a card to preview the voice (if available)
  Click "Select this voice" to confirm

The voice you pick is what the audio step uses for the final render.


================================================================
STEP 4: AUDIO
================================================================

The script is rendered with your chosen voice using ElevenLabs. The
audio appears as a waveform you can play, pause, and click anywhere
to seek.

Two buttons:

  Regenerate    — render a new take. ElevenLabs is non-deterministic
                  so the same script with the same voice produces
                  slight variations.

  Approve       — lock in this audio. The Final Package view appears.


================================================================
FINAL PACKAGE
================================================================

After approving the audio, the Final Package view replaces the
step view. You'll see:

  Approved Copy    — headline, caption, CTA
  Approved Image   — full size
  Voiceover        — script text, voice name, waveform player
  Director's Notes — a clean audit trail of every refinement and
                     critique you applied across all four steps,
                     in the order you made them

At the top, two buttons:

  Edit any step    — scrolls up to the stepper so you can click any
                     approved step and revise. The Final Package
                     re-renders once everything is re-approved.

  Download package — produces a zip file containing:

                         copy.txt           — the headline, caption,
                                              and CTA as plain text
                         image.{png|jpg}    — the approved image
                         voiceover.mp3      — the audio file
                         director-notes.md  — the same audit trail
                                              as on screen, in
                                              markdown form

The zip is ready to hand off to a campaign manager, drop into an ad
platform, or share with a teammate for review.


================================================================
BACKTRACKING (THE TOOL'S BEST TRICK)
================================================================

At any point, click any approved step in the stepper at the top of
the screen. You'll be returned to that step with all your variants
and choices intact. Re-pick a different variant, or refine again.

When you re-approve and the chain rebuilds, you may see a small pill
above the variant grid that reads:

    "Restored from your earlier choices — no regeneration"

This means the demo recognized you've already explored this exact
path and is showing you the cached result rather than re-generating
(and re-billing your API). Exploring multiple branches costs you
only the first traversal of each branch.


================================================================
IF SOMETHING GOES WRONG
================================================================

The demo is built to recover gracefully from common issues. You'll
see a calm error message, not a technical stack trace.

  "Your X key isn't being accepted"
      Open Settings and re-paste the key. Sometimes a stray space or
      newline gets copied along with it. Click "Try again" after
      pasting.

  "X is busy. Try again in a few seconds."
      The provider is rate-limiting briefly. Wait 10 seconds and
      click "Try again."

  "Couldn't reach X. Check your internet and try again."
      A network blip. Click "Try again." If it persists, switch to
      a different network briefly.

  "This voice isn't available on your ElevenLabs account."
      A "Pick a different voice" button appears below the error.
      Click it to return to the voice picker, where you can choose
      one of your account voices.

  Anything else
      A "Try again" button is always present. If repeated retries
      fail, refresh the page and start over — your brief and most of
      your variants will be restored from your browser's session
      storage.


================================================================
PRIVACY AND COST
================================================================

Your API keys live only in your browser's session storage. They are
never sent to any server other than the four service providers above
(OpenAI, fal.ai, ElevenLabs, Anthropic). They clear when you close
the browser tab.

You pay your own provider for your own usage. A typical full run of
all four steps with no refinements costs a small fraction of a
dollar across all four providers combined. Heavy iteration with many
refines pushes that to a few cents. The most expensive single call
is the audio render (a few cents per take). Copy, image, and script
generations are well under a cent each.

The demo's owner does not see your keys, your brief, your variants,
or your final assets. Everything happens in your browser.


================================================================
SUGGESTED FIRST RUN
================================================================

  1. Open the URL above.
  2. Click "Try the sample brief instead" on the welcome screen.
     Watch the full pipeline restore in under a second.
  3. Click any approved step in the stepper. Try a refine like
     "more aggressive, less corporate." Watch new variants render.
  4. Open Settings, paste your four API keys, validate them.
  5. Click "New brief" in the top right.
  6. Fill in your own product, audience, and angle.
  7. Walk through copy, image, script, voice, audio.
  8. At the image step, try the Critique button on at least one
     variant. Read the prose feedback. Apply it.
  9. Approve audio.
 10. Click Download package on the Final Package view.

Total time: about ten minutes. The zip will be in your downloads
folder when you're done.


================================================================
QUESTIONS
================================================================

The demo is intentionally self-explanatory; everything you need to
know is surfaced at the moment you need it. If you get stuck, the
"Try again" button on any error gives you a path forward, and
clicking any approved step in the stepper lets you backtrack
without losing work.
